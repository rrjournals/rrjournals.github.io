Canadians in Cuba (Shocking, I know)

This was a post-Asia trip I did with some high school friends. I went back to Toronto for a concert after going through Asia (which was actually the best night of these crazy five weeks of traveling... story for another day). Anyway, we stayed at a resort in Varadero which was an awesome time because of all the freestyling we did and the nights out we had, but I don’t know if I would do it again the same way. To me, the only thing that made the resort worth it was the all-inclusive drinks but alcohol was cheap in Cuba anyway (a bottle of Havanna Club costs like 8 USD). The food was real average (but all you can eat) and I met more Canadians in Cuba than I would on an average day in Toronto. On top of that, we would leave the resort at night anyway and so if I were to go back, I would definitely stay in a hostel or homestay to actually embrace Cuban culture... because let’s be honest, Cuban culture isn’t Canadians drinking on a beach all day.

However, while we were there, we managed to wake up early enough one day to go on an excursion off the resort. We went snorkeling (and I did not feed any fish... this time.) and saw some of the scenery in the area and it was unreal!
